GLP-1 Buyer's Guide: What You Need to Know

What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) receptor agonists are medications originally developed for type 2 diabetes that have proven highly effective for weight loss. The two main medications are:

  • Semaglutide (Wegovy/Ozempic): Shown to help people lose 15-20% of body weight on average
  • Tirzepatide (Zepbound/Mounjaro): Newer medication with even better results, averaging 20-25% weight loss

These medications work by reducing appetite, slowing digestion, and regulating blood sugar levels.

Brand vs. Compounded: What's the Difference?

Brand-name medications (Wegovy, Zepbound) are FDA-approved but expensive ($1,000-1,500/month without insurance).

Compounded versions contain the same active ingredients but are made by specialized pharmacies. They cost $150-300/month and are legal when brand versions are in shortage (current status).

Most online providers offer compounded versions, making treatment affordable for more people.

How to Choose the Right Provider

Consider these factors when selecting a GLP-1 program:

  • Price: Total monthly cost including consultation, medication, and shipping
  • Medication options: Semaglutide only vs. both Semaglutide and Tirzepatide
  • Support level: Coaching, nutritionist access, or basic medical oversight
  • Program approach: Medication-only vs. comprehensive lifestyle program
  • Delivery speed: How quickly you'll receive your medication
  • Contracts: Month-to-month vs. required commitments

Am I a Good Candidate?

GLP-1 medications are typically prescribed for adults who meet these criteria:

  • BMI of 30 or higher (obesity), OR
  • BMI of 27 or higher with weight-related health conditions (high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, etc.)
  • Have tried diet and exercise without sufficient success
  • No contraindications (personal/family history of certain cancers, severe gastrointestinal disease)

What to Expect: The Process

  1. Online Assessment (Day 1): Complete medical questionnaire about your health history, weight loss goals, and current medications
  2. Doctor Review (1-2 days): Licensed physician reviews your information and determines if you're a good candidate
  3. Prescription & Shipment (2-7 days): If approved, medication is prescribed and shipped to your door
  4. Starting Treatment: Most providers start with a low dose and gradually increase over 8-12 weeks
  5. Ongoing Monitoring: Regular check-ins to monitor progress and adjust dosage as needed

Common Side Effects

Most people tolerate GLP-1 medications well, but side effects can include:

  • Nausea (usually temporary, decreases over time)
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Decreased appetite
  • Fatigue
  • Headaches

Starting with a low dose and increasing gradually helps minimize side effects. Most side effects improve after the first few weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much weight can I expect to lose?

Clinical studies show average weight loss of 15-20% of body weight with Semaglutide and 20-25% with Tirzepatide over 12-18 months. Individual results vary based on starting weight, adherence, and lifestyle factors.

Is online GLP-1 treatment safe?

Yes, when prescribed by licensed physicians and obtained from legitimate providers. All providers we review require medical evaluation and ongoing monitoring. Ensure you choose a provider with licensed US healthcare professionals.

Will my insurance cover it?

Some insurance plans cover brand-name GLP-1 medications for weight loss, but many don't. Compounded versions (what most online providers offer) are typically not covered by insurance. However, the out-of-pocket cost for compounded medications ($150-300/month) is often less than insurance copays for brand versions.

How long do I need to take it?

GLP-1 medications work while you're taking them. Most people see results within 8-12 weeks and continue treatment for 12-24 months to reach their goal weight. Some choose to continue at a maintenance dose long-term.

What happens if I stop taking it?

Weight regain is common after stopping GLP-1 medications. The key is using the medication as a tool to develop sustainable eating and exercise habits. Many providers offer transition plans to help maintain results.

Are compounded medications as effective as brand-name?

Compounded medications contain the same active ingredients (Semaglutide or Tirzepatide) in the same dosages as brand-name versions. While they haven't undergone the same FDA approval process, many people report similar results at a fraction of the cost.

How do I inject the medication?

GLP-1 medications are injected subcutaneously (under the skin) once weekly using a small needle, similar to insulin. Most providers include detailed instructions and injection supplies. The process is quick (30 seconds) and relatively painless.

Can I use GLP-1 if I have diabetes?

Yes, GLP-1 medications were originally developed for type 2 diabetes. However, you should inform your provider about your diabetes and any medications you're taking, as dosing may need adjustment.
